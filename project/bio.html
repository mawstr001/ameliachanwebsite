The Artist & The Teacher

A leader's instinct,
a teacher's clarity.

Hong Kong · New York

Amelia Chan is a violinist whose playing has been described as the vibrant soul of the ensemble — an artist who leads from the concertmaster's chair and teaches from first principles. As a chamber musician she served as first violinist of the Montclaire String Quartet and has collaborated with guitarist Sharon Isbin, accordionist Richard Galliano, violinist Lara St. John, the Ying Quartet, members of the Vienna Philharmonic, and the Jupiter Symphony Chamber Players.

She has appeared as soloist with orchestras across the Americas and Asia, and shared the stage as co-soloist with flutist Sir James Galway. Her playing has been heard on air across WQXR New York, WQED Pittsburgh, West Virginia Public Broadcasting, BBC Radio Scotland, and RTHK Radio 4 Hong Kong.

On Teaching

Technique through
the whole body.

Biomechanics · Discernment · Simplicity

As an educator, Amelia approaches technique through the lens of whole-body biomechanics — on the principle that playing an instrument must be relational to the body and how it moves, rather than a static, one-size-fits-all method.

She believes in a focused, deep education that goes beyond rote training, where the student learns discernment and critical thinking while sifting through the layers of intellect needed to decipher the musical art — to reach the natural, joyful simplicity of music-making.

Amelia holds undergraduate, graduate, and post-graduate degrees from the Mannes College of Music and Manhattan School of Music, and began her violin studies in the junior school at the Hong Kong Academy for Performing Arts. Her major teachers included Albert Markov, Shirley Givens, Glenn Dicterow, Sheryl Staples, and double-bassist Julius Levine.
